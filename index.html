Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a vibrant, one-pot West African dish made with long-grain rice, tomatoes, onions, and a blend of spices like thyme, curry, and scotch bonnet peppers. Often cooked with vegetables, meat, or fish, it has a rich, spicy flavor and bright red-orange color from tomato paste or stew. Popular in countries like Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal, it’s a staple at celebrations and gatherings, with regional variations sparking friendly debates over the best recipe.

Recipie Information

  • Preparation time: About 15–20 minutes
  • Cooking time: Approximately 45–60 minutes
  • Number of servings: 4-6
  • Difficulty level: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • Rice: 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice (e.g., Uncle Ben’s or Golden Sella)
  • Tomatoes: 4–5 medium fresh tomatoes or 1 can (14 oz) tomato puree
  • Tomato Paste: 2–3 tablespoons
  • Onions: 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • Red Bell Pepper: 1–2, blended or finely chopped
  • Scotch Bonnet Peppers: 1–2 (adjust for spice preference), blended or finely chopped
  • Vegetable Oil: ¼–½ cup (or palm oil for traditional flavor)
  • Garlic: 2–3 cloves, minced (optional)
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, grated (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1–2 bay leaves
  • Stock Cubes: 2–3 (e.g., Maggi or Knorr) or 1–2 teaspoons seasoning powder
  • Chicken or Vegetable Stock: 3–4 cups (or water)
  • Protein (optional): 1 lb chicken, beef, fish, or shrimp, pre-cooked or fried
  • Salt: To taste

Instructions

  1. Heat ¼–½ cup vegetable oil (or palm oil) in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent, about 3–4 minutes.
  3. Stir in 2–3 tablespoons tomato paste and cook for 2–3 minutes, stirring to prevent burning.
  4. Add the blended tomato-pepper puree, garlic, and ginger (if using). Cook for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens and the oil begins to separate (the “stew” base).
  5. Add 1 teaspoon thyme, 1 teaspoon curry powder, 1–2 bay leaves, 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional), and 2–3 stock cubes (or seasoning powder). Stir well.
  6. Adjust with salt to taste. Cook for another 2–3 minutes to blend flavors.
  7. Add the rinsed rice to the pot, stirring to coat it evenly with the tomato base.
  8. Pour in 3–4 cups of chicken/vegetable stock or water (ensure liquid just covers the rice). Stir gently.
  9. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid (use foil if needed to trap steam) and simmer for 20–30 minutes. Check occasionally; add a little more liquid if the rice is still hard.
  10. If using vegetables (e.g., carrots, peas, or green beans), stir them in about 10 minutes before the rice is fully cooked.
  11. Add pre-cooked protein (chicken, beef, shrimp, or fish) at this stage, nestling it into the rice to warm through.
  12. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Check rice for doneness; it should be soft but not mushy.
  13. If you prefer the smoky “party jollof” flavor, let the rice slightly “burn” at the bottom (low heat, 2–3 minutes, without stirring).
  14. Remove from heat, fluff the rice with a fork, and discard bay leaves.
  15. Serve hot with a side of fried plantain, salad, or coleslaw.

Tips

For best results, use a heavy-bottomed pot to prevent burning, and avoid stirring too much during cooking to keep the rice grains intact. Cooking times may vary depending on rice type and heat level.
